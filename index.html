Our Story

Founded in 1963, Adler Publishing House began as a small family-run press in New York City, devoted to showcasing emerging voices in fiction and cultural criticism. In its early years, Adler published a series of now-iconic essay collections that helped define a generation of American intellectualism.

Throughout the 1980s and 1990s, Adler expanded its reach to include translated works, poetry, and academic nonfiction, earning a reputation for editorial integrity and artistic risk. While many independent presses consolidated, Adler remained fiercely autonomous, guided by a commitment to authorship over all.

Today, Adler Publishing House continues to champion distinctive storytelling. We publish across genres — from political memoirs to experimental fiction — and remain a platform for writers who challenge conventions and deepen our understanding of the world.

Meet Our Team

Clara Hastings

Editor-in-Chief

Clara Hastings is a Yale graduate and acclaimed editor who, during her tenure as junior editor at the prestigious Sotheby’s Publishing, championed multiple National Book Award finalists. Recognized for elevating bold, genre-defying voices, she was named one of the “Top 30 People to Watch in Literature Under 30.”

Margot Ellison

Creative Director

Margot Ellison is a Harvard alumna and former junior director of fiction at Random House, where she developed Pulitzer Prize–winning titles. A MacDowell fellow, she’s celebrated for her visionary taste and dedication to amplifying underrepresented voices.

Sabine Cho

Editor

Sabine Cho is a Northwestern graduate and former editor at Christie's and The New Yorker Books. She’s known for championing award-winning, boundary-pushing authors and currently serves as a judge for the Jean Stein Book Award.

Lena Okafor

Editor

Lena Okafor, after completing her undergraduate at Yale and receiving her MFA in literature from New York University, she served as the assistant to the senior editor of Penguin Random House Publishing. Following that, she became head of the literary New York Times Best Books of the Year.

Diana Morales

Editor-in-Residence

Diana Morales is a junior editor at Sotheby’s, where she assisted the publishing of Pulitzer Prize winners and #1 New York Times bestsellers. A Harvard graduate and Fulbright scholar, she’s known for her sharp eye and fearless literary instincts.
